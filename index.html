A Small City with Big Character

Nestled in the coastal plain of southeast Georgia, Statesboro is the county seat of Bulloch County and home to Georgia Southern University. With a population of around 35,000, it blends small-town Southern charm with the energy of a university town.

Founded in 1803, the city has grown from a rural courthouse town into a regional hub for education, agriculture, and culture. Its downtown district preserves historic architecture while welcoming new restaurants, shops, and creative spaces.

The name itself tells a story — honoring the states that ratified the Constitution, it carries a thread of American idealism woven into red Georgia clay.

Local Landmarks

🏛

Bulloch County Courthouse

The historic courthouse anchors downtown Statesboro, a symbol of the city's civic heritage dating back to the early 1900s. The surrounding square hosts seasonal markets and community events.

🎓

Georgia Southern University

Home of the Eagles. Georgia Southern's sprawling campus features Sweetheart Circle, the iconic Rotunda, Paulson Stadium, and a Wildlife Center housing native raptors and reptiles.

🏘

Downtown Statesboro

A revitalized Main Street district with locally-owned shops, restaurants, and galleries. The Blue Room hosts live music, and seasonal festivals fill the streets throughout the year.

🌾

Farmers Markets

Bulloch County's agricultural roots shine at the Statesboro Mainstreet Farmers Market, where local growers sell produce, honey, pecans, and homemade goods every Saturday morning.

🏟

Paulson Stadium

Named after Allen E. Paulson, this 25,000-seat stadium is where Georgia Southern football comes alive. The Eagles' triple-option offense has a legendary history in college football.

🦅

Center for Wildlife Education

Located on GSU's campus, this facility houses native Georgia wildlife including bald eagles, hawks, owls, and reptiles. Free educational programs run year-round.

Culture & Community

Music Heritage

Blind Willie McTell, the legendary blues musician, was born near Statesboro in 1898. His song "Statesboro Blues" — later covered by the Allman Brothers Band — put the city's name in the American songbook forever.

Southern Cuisine

From Vandy's BBQ to local diners serving up biscuits and gravy, Statesboro's food scene runs deep in Southern tradition. The university influx has also brought diverse options — Thai, Mexican, Mediterranean, and more.

Martial Arts Community

Statesboro has an active martial arts community spanning multiple disciplines. Local schools teach everything from traditional Korean arts like Tang Soo Do to Filipino martial arts like Arnis, fostering discipline and community connection.

Arts & Theatre

The Averitt Center for the Arts serves as Statesboro's cultural hub, offering gallery exhibitions, performing arts, and community programs. GSU's theater and music departments bring world-class performances to the area.

Nature & Outdoors

Mill Creek Regional Park

A sprawling park with walking trails, sports fields, a disc golf course, and a splash pad. The go-to spot for Statesboro families year-round.

S&S Greenway Trail

Built on the old Savannah & Statesboro Railway corridor, this paved trail winds through the city — perfect for running, cycling, or a quiet evening walk.

Georgia Southern Gardens

The university's botanical garden features native plants, a heritage garden, and winding paths beneath towering live oaks draped in Spanish moss.

Plan Your Visit

Getting Here

Statesboro sits about 50 miles northwest of Savannah along US-301 and Highway 67. Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport (SAV) is the nearest major airport.

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring (March–May): Mild weather, azaleas in bloom, outdoor festivals
  • Fall (Sept–Nov): Football season, cooler temps, harvest markets
  • Kiwanis Ogeechee Fair: Annual October tradition with rides, food, and livestock shows
